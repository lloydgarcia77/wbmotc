Our Staff

Federico Ancheta.

Owner, MOT, RMT Associate

Federico Ancheta is one of the Owners of Wood Buffalo Massage and Osteopathic Therapy Clinic. Freddie was born and raised in Northern Philippines, where he was a licensed Physiotherapist since 2006.

He is also a licensed Physiotherapist in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia and Qatar.

He, together with his wife Joy and eldest son Charles, moved to Fort McMurray four years ago. And now, he is blessed to have two more children named Harry and Maria Elizabeth.

Freddie had a wide variety of jobs: being a cashier, kitchen helper, warehouseman, butcher, merchandiser, and industrial cleaner before graduating as a Manual Osteopathic Therapist.

Freddie is now a Manual Osteopathic Therapist, graduated from Manual Osteopathic College of Canada. Also, he finished his Massage Practitioner Program in Alberta College of Massage Therapy with an "Honors with Distinction" award.

Currently, Freddie is now on his Massage Therapy Diploma at ACMT and will finish his program very soon as well as his Physiotherapy Credentialing.

Joy Ancheta

Co-Owner, MOT, RMT Associate

Joy Ancheta is the co-owner of Wood Buffalo Massage and Osteopathic Therapy Clinic. She was born in the Southern Part of Luzon, Philippines and was raised in Northern Philippines.

Joy is a licensed Physiotherapist in Qatar.

She has a passion in cooking Asian cuisines and in helping other people.

She previously owned a day home in Fort McMurray and plans to reopen it in the future.

Joy is a Manual Osteopathic Therapist and she graduated at Manual Osteopathic College of Canada. Also, she graduated as a Massage Practitioner with a "Honors with Distinction" award from Alberta College of Massage Therapy.

Joy was awarded by ACMT as an Ambassador to inspire others.

Currently, she is finishing her Massage Therapy Diploma at ACMT - Fort McMurray very soon.

Charles Joseph Ancheta

Receptionist

Charles was born and raised at Northern Philippines. He was the eldest son of Freddie and Joy.

Charles is a loving son and brother to his family. He helps out to his family when he can and he always provide care to his siblings when he is at home.

He is a consistent honor student since his elementary school years. Also, he was a recipient of the Alberta Great Kids Award in the year 2019.

Currently, Charles is in his last year of Senior High School at Ecole McTavish High School and is a part of RMWB's MACOY.
